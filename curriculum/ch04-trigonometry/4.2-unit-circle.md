# A Point on the Circle Makes a Triangle

<div style="display: flex; gap: 24px; height: 78%;">
<div style="flex: 0 0 38%; display: flex; flex-direction: column; justify-content: center;">

Pick any point on the unit circle at angle **θ**.

Drop a vertical line down to the x-axis — you've just made a **right triangle**.

<div style="margin: 1rem 0; line-height: 2;">

- The <span style="color: var(--color-cos)">**horizontal leg**</span> has length **x**
- The <span style="color: var(--color-sin)">**vertical leg**</span> has length **y**
- The **hypotenuse** is the radius: **r = 1**

</div>

<DiscoveryPrompt
  question="If the hypotenuse is 1, what does the Pythagorean theorem tell us about x and y?"
  hint="x² + y² = r² = 1² = 1. This is always true for any point on the unit circle — at every angle."
/>

</div>

# The Triangle Reveals Sine and Cosine

<div style="display: flex; gap: 24px; height: 78%;">
<div style="flex: 0 0 38%; display: flex; flex-direction: column; justify-content: center;">

That same right triangle connects directly to **SOHCAHTOA**.

Since the hypotenuse is **always 1** on the unit circle, something powerful happens:

<div style="margin: 1rem 0; line-height: 2.2; font-family: var(--font-mono); font-size: 0.95rem;">

<span style="color: var(--color-cos)">cos θ</span> = adj / hyp = <span style="color: var(--color-cos)">x</span> / 1 = <span style="color: var(--color-cos)">**x**</span>

<span style="color: var(--color-sin)">sin θ</span> = opp / hyp = <span style="color: var(--color-sin)">y</span> / 1 = <span style="color: var(--color-sin)">**y**</span>

</div>

<DiscoveryPrompt
  question="Why is the unit circle (r = 1) so special for trigonometry?"
  hint="When r = 1, the trig ratios simplify to just the coordinates. The x-coordinate IS cosine, the y-coordinate IS sine. No division needed."
/>

</div>

</div>

# What Did You Discover?

<div style="margin: 1rem 0;">

As θ increases from 0° to 360°, the **height** of the point on the circle — that is, **sin θ** — traces out a smooth wave.

</div>

<DiscoveryPrompt
  question="At what angles does sin θ reach its maximum? Its minimum? Where is it zero?"
  hint="Maximum at 90° (sin 90° = 1). Minimum at 270° (sin 270° = −1). Zero at 0°, 180°, and 360°."
/>

<div style="margin-top: 1.5rem;">

<DiscoveryPrompt

  hint="The cosine wave has the same shape but is shifted 90° to the left — it 'leads' the sine wave. Go back to the interactive and try it!"

# The Sine Function

The pattern you just traced is the **sine function**:

<div style="text-align: center; font-size: 1.4rem; margin: 1.5rem 0; font-family: var(--font-mono);">
  f(θ) = <span style="color: var(--color-sin)">sin θ</span>
</div>

| Property | Value |
|:---|:---|
| **Domain** | All real numbers |
| **Range** | [−1, 1] |
| **Period** | 360° = 2π |
| **Maximum** | sin 90° = 1 |
| **Minimum** | sin 270° = −1 |
| **Zeros** | 0°, 180°, 360°, ... |

---

# Key Angles

<div style="margin: 1rem 0; font-size: 0.95rem;">

| θ (degrees) | θ (radians) | sin θ | cos θ |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 0° | 0 | 0 | 1 |
| 30° | π/6 | 1/2 | √3/2 |
| 45° | π/4 | √2/2 | √2/2 |
| 60° | π/3 | √3/2 | 1/2 |
| 90° | π/2 | 1 | 0 |
| 180° | π | 0 | −1 |
| 270° | 3π/2 | −1 | 0 |
| 360° | 2π | 0 | 1 |

</div>

<DiscoveryPrompt
  question="Do you notice a relationship between sin θ and cos θ at complementary angles (angles that add to 90°)?"
  hint="sin 30° = cos 60° = 1/2 and sin 60° = cos 30° = √3/2. In general, sin θ = cos(90° − θ). This is the cofunction identity!"
/>
